Dental Implants vs. Bridges: Which is Right for You?

Everything you need to know before choosing your tooth replacement option

Published: May 25, 2026 | By Nu Shine Dental Team

Losing a tooth can be distressing, but modern dentistry offers excellent solutions. Two of the most popular options are dental implants and bridges. Here's a comprehensive comparison to help you decide.

What is a Dental Implant?

A dental implant is a titanium post surgically placed into the jawbone, acting as an artificial tooth root. A crown is then placed on top, creating a natural-looking replacement tooth. Implants fuse with the bone through osseointegration, providing unmatched stability.

What is a Dental Bridge?

A bridge consists of one or more artificial teeth anchored in place by crowns on the adjacent natural teeth. The neighbouring teeth are filed down to support the bridge, which fills the gap left by the missing tooth.

Comparison at a Glance

FactorDental ImplantDental Bridge
Longevity20+ years or lifetime5-15 years
ProcedureSurgical (2-3 visits)Non-surgical (2 visits)
CostHigher upfrontLower upfront
Bone PreservationStimulates bone growthNo bone preservation
Adjacent TeethUnaffectedMust be filed down
Recovery1-2 weeks initialMinimal

Ultimately, the best choice depends on your oral health, budget, and personal preferences. A consultation with your dentist will help determine the ideal solution for your smile.
